# Analytics Basics

This skill should be used when users want to check campaign performance, understand marketing metrics, interpret analytics dashboards, or measure ROI. Trigger phrases include: "check metrics", "campaign performance", "analytics", "insights", "ROI", "how is my campaign doing", "what do these numbers mean".

## Key Marketing Metrics

### Reach Metrics
- **Reach**: Number of unique people who saw your content
- **Impressions**: Total number of times content was displayed (includes repeats)
- **Frequency**: Average times each person saw your content (Impressions ÷ Reach)

### Engagement Metrics
- **Engagement Rate**: (Total Engagements ÷ Reach) × 100
- **Likes/Reactions**: Positive responses to content
- **Comments**: User responses and conversations
- **Shares/Retweets**: Content shared by users
- **Saves**: Content bookmarked for later (Instagram)
- **Clicks**: Interactions with links or CTAs

### Conversion Metrics
- **Click-Through Rate (CTR)**: (Clicks ÷ Impressions) × 100
- **Conversion Rate**: (Conversions ÷ Clicks) × 100
- **Cost Per Click (CPC)**: Total Spend ÷ Total Clicks
- **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)**: Total Spend ÷ Total Conversions
- **Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)**: Revenue ÷ Ad Spend

### Growth Metrics
- **Follower Growth Rate**: (New Followers - Unfollows) ÷ Starting Followers × 100
- **Profile Visits**: People who visited your profile
- **Website Clicks**: Clicks to external website from profile

## Benchmark Ranges

### Good Performance Benchmarks

| Metric | Poor | Average | Good | Excellent |
|--------|------|---------|------|-----------|
| Engagement Rate (Instagram) | <1% | 1-3% | 3-6% | >6% |
| Engagement Rate (Facebook) | <0.5% | 0.5-1% | 1-2% | >2% |
| Engagement Rate (LinkedIn) | <1% | 1-2% | 2-4% | >4% |
| CTR (Social Ads) | <0.5% | 0.5-1% | 1-2% | >2% |
| Email Open Rate | <15% | 15-25% | 25-35% | >35% |
| Video View Rate | <25% | 25-50% | 50-75% | >75% |

### Industry Variations
- B2B typically has lower engagement but higher conversion value
- Consumer brands see higher engagement, lower per-conversion value
- Local businesses often have higher engagement from smaller audiences


- Compare to your average (not just benchmarks)

- Check which content types work best
- Note posting times that got better reach

## Google Analytics Basics

### Accessing Google Analytics
**URL**: https://analytics.google.com

### Key Reports for Marketing

**Acquisition → Traffic Acquisition**:
- Shows where visitors come from
- Filter by "Social" to see social media traffic
- Compare channels performance

**Engagement → Pages and Screens**:
- Which pages people visit
- Time on page
- Bounce rate

**Acquisition → User Acquisition**:
- New vs returning users
- First visit channel attribution

### Key GA4 Metrics

**Users**: Unique visitors to your site
**Sessions**: Total visits (one user can have multiple)
**Engaged Sessions**: Sessions lasting >10 sec or with conversion
**Engagement Rate**: Engaged Sessions ÷ Total Sessions
**Average Engagement Time**: Time actively on site

### UTM Tracking

Add UTM parameters to links for better tracking:

```
https://yoursite.com/page?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=spring_sale
```

**Parameters**:
- `utm_source`: Platform (instagram, facebook, linkedin)
- `utm_medium`: Channel type (social, email, cpc)
- `utm_campaign`: Campaign name
- `utm_content`: Specific ad or post (optional)

### Common Questions Answered

**"Is my reach good?"**
Compare to:
1. Your own average (most important)
2. Your follower count (should reach 10-30% organically)
3. Industry benchmarks

**"Why did engagement drop?"**
Check:
1. Algorithm changes (platform-wide issue)
2. Content quality/type changes
3. Posting time/frequency changes
4. Seasonal factors

**"How do I improve these numbers?"**
Focus on:
1. One metric at a time
2. Test one variable at a time
3. Give tests 2-4 weeks to show results
4. Document what you change

## Analytics Glossary

| Term | Simple Explanation |
|------|-------------------|
| Reach | How many different people saw you |
| Impressions | How many times you were seen total |
| Engagement | Any interaction with your content |
| CTR | How often people click when they see you |
| Conversion | When someone does what you wanted |
| Bounce Rate | People who left immediately |
| ROI | Money made vs money spent |
| ROAS | Revenue from ads vs ad cost |
