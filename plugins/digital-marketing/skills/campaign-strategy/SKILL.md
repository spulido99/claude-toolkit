# Campaign Strategy

This skill should be used when users ask about planning marketing campaigns, defining target audiences, setting campaign goals, choosing platforms, or allocating budgets. Trigger phrases include: "plan a campaign", "marketing strategy", "define my audience", "set campaign goals", "which platforms should I use", "how much should I spend".

## Campaign Objectives

There are three main types of marketing objectives. Choose based on business goals:

### Awareness
- **Goal**: Get your brand seen by more people
- **Best for**: New businesses, product launches, entering new markets
- **Key metrics**: Reach, impressions, video views
- **Typical budget split**: 70% reach campaigns, 30% engagement

### Engagement
- **Goal**: Build relationships and community
- **Best for**: Building loyalty, increasing followers, generating conversations
- **Key metrics**: Likes, comments, shares, saves, followers gained
- **Typical budget split**: 60% engagement posts, 40% community interaction

### Conversion
- **Goal**: Drive specific actions (sales, signups, downloads)
- **Best for**: E-commerce, lead generation, app installs
- **Key metrics**: Click-through rate, conversion rate, cost per acquisition
- **Typical budget split**: 50% conversion ads, 30% retargeting, 20% awareness

## Audience Definition

Define your target audience using these dimensions:

### Demographics
- Age range (e.g., 25-34)
- Gender (if relevant)
- Location (country, city, or region)
- Income level
- Education
- Occupation

### Psychographics
- Interests and hobbies
- Values and beliefs
- Lifestyle choices
- Pain points and challenges
- Goals and aspirations

### Behavioral
- Online behavior (platforms used, time spent)
- Purchase behavior
- Brand interactions
- Content preferences

### Audience Statement Template
"My target audience is [demographics] who [psychographic trait] and struggle with [pain point]. They want to [goal/aspiration] and typically [behavioral trait]."

Example: "My target audience is small business owners aged 30-45 in Latin America who value efficiency and struggle with time management. They want to grow their business sustainably and typically research solutions on Instagram and LinkedIn."

## Budget Guidelines

### Small Budget ($100-500/month)
- Focus on ONE platform
- Prioritize organic content with minimal paid boost
- Test different content types before scaling
- Recommended: 80% organic, 20% paid

### Medium Budget ($500-2000/month)
- Expand to 2-3 platforms
- Consistent paid promotion
- A/B testing budget included
- Recommended: 60% paid campaigns, 40% content creation

### Large Budget ($2000+/month)
- Full multi-platform presence
- Professional content creation
- Advanced targeting and retargeting
- Recommended: 70% paid, 20% content, 10% testing

### Budget Allocation by Objective
| Objective | Content Creation | Paid Ads | Testing |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|---------|
| Awareness | 30% | 60% | 10% |
| Engagement | 50% | 40% | 10% |
| Conversion | 20% | 65% | 15% |

## Platform Selection

Choose platforms based on audience and objective:

| Platform | Best For | Audience Age | Content Type |
|----------|----------|--------------|--------------|
| Instagram | Visual brands, lifestyle, B2C | 18-44 | Images, Reels, Stories |
| Facebook | Local business, community, 35+ | 25-55 | Posts, Groups, Events |
| LinkedIn | B2B, professional services | 25-54 | Articles, thought leadership |
| TikTok | Young audience, viral content | 16-34 | Short video, trends |
| Twitter/X | News, tech, real-time | 25-49 | Text, conversations |

### Awareness KPIs
- Reach (unique people who saw content)

## Campaign Timeline

### Quick Campaign (1-2 weeks)
- Day 1-2: Strategy and content creation
- Day 3-7: Launch and monitor
- Day 8-14: Optimize and report

### Standard Campaign (1 month)
- Week 1: Strategy, audience research, content planning
- Week 2: Content creation and scheduling
- Week 3-4: Launch, monitor, optimize

### Long-term Campaign (3+ months)
- Month 1: Foundation (strategy, initial content, testing)
- Month 2: Scale (increase budget on winners, new content)
- Month 3+: Optimize (refine targeting, expand platforms)

## Strategy Document Structure

When defining a campaign, document these elements:

1. **Campaign Name**: Descriptive name for reference
2. **Objective**: Primary goal (awareness/engagement/conversion)
3. **Target Audience**: Detailed audience description
4. **Platforms**: Selected social platforms
5. **Budget**: Total and allocation
6. **Timeline**: Start/end dates, key milestones
7. **KPIs**: 3-5 measurable metrics
8. **Content Themes**: Main topics and messaging
9. **Language**: English, Spanish, or both
